Laminate Flooring
 
Laminate flooring is a tongue-and-groove interlocking flooring system that is installed over the top of the existing hard surface including plywood, concrete slab, sheet vinyl, hardwood flooring and more. In a typical floating installation, only the joints are glued or attached; the boards are not attached to the subfloor. Laminate allows for the same look as hardwood floor while maintaining a more affordable pricetag.

Engineered Wood
 
Engineered wood is made up of layers of plywood core and a top layer of solid wood. The main difference between engineered wood and solid wood is this: when you want to resurface, or refinish, the wood years later, engineered wood will only have a top layer of solid wood to sand through and then you will run into either the nails that hold the flooring down or the plywood core. Solid wood flooring, however, expands and contracts with the air's humidity, whereas engineered wood is more solid and less susceptible to the environmental changes. The installation can be glued, stapled, nailed or floating.

Pre-finished wood
 
Pre-finished wood advantages include minimum fuss and easy installation. Pre-finished hardwood is a good choice for those who wish to install their hardwood floor without having to do any sanding or finishing. Pre-finished hardwood floors are therefore ideal for the individual who wishes to save time and bother by being able to enjoy the benefits of a new hardwood floor immediately after it's installed. Unlike engineered wood, pre-finished solid wood can be refinished.

Unfinished wood
 

Unfinished wood is a raw wood that is fresh-cut from the mill and delivered without a finish. This allows the homeowner to specify what type of stain and finish they prefer. The unfinished wood flooring is valued by the uniformity of the finish. Pre-finished boards are finished with a seal that goes between individual hardwood planks. With an unfinished fllor, the finish is added over the surface of a hardwood floor as a whole after the boards have been installed. This benefits a more uniform finish and a greater moisture-proof seal.
