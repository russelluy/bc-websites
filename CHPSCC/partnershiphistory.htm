History
The Community Health Partnership of Santa Clara County (Partnership) was founded in 1993 in response to community concern for the viability of community-based primary care clinics. The community clinics are valued as an essential “safety net” for the county's most vulnerable residents.

The mission of the Partnership is to expand access to health services for the medically underserved, encourage prevention and health promotion through service, education and advocacy. The Partnership is committed to developing affordable health services that meet the needs of our diverse multi-cultural community.

The membership of the Community Health Partnership is comprised of nine primary care organizations and two governmental agencies. The Partnership member organizations are Asian Americans for Community Involvment, East Valley Community Clinic, Gardner Family Health Network, Indian Health Center of Santa Clara Valley, May View Community Health Center, Planned Parenthood Mar Monte, Ravenswood Family Health Center, San Jose Foothill Community Clinic and School Health Clinics. The membership also includes the City of San Jose Municipal Health Services Program and the Santa Clara Valley Health and Hospital System, Public Health, and Ambulatory and Community Services System.

The member community clinics were founded to serve medically indigent and underserved populations. Their commitment to this mission holds strong to this day. All of the clinics have multi cultural staff. They provide primary care services and an array of programs, including but not limited to, dental care, mental health, family planning, case management, health education, nutrition (through the Federal Women, Infant, and Children program), outreach, and prenatal services. With their combined budgets of over $40 million, they provide services through more than 300,000 encounters annually at twenty-six clinic sites throughout the county.

The Community Health Partnership has been able to forge a public-private partnership unique among community clinic organizations in the State, drawing participation from a variety of organizations which have normally not worked together. The Partnership has a track record of effective collaboration to improve preventive health services for “at risk” populations and successful community outreach.
