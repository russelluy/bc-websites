Community Diabetes Project: Diabetes & Chronic Conditions Self Management Classes  

Self-Management Diabetes Classes
The Community Diabetes Project, along with the assistance of the Stanford University Patient Education and Research Center , developed a diabetes self-management course for diabetics and their families or support persons. The course, comprised of six 2½-hour classes, is taught in Spanish at clinics and community centers throughout the county of Santa Clara . The classes meet once a week and are led by two trained and certified Peer Educators (Promotores de Salud) who are members of the client population. The topics covered include nutrition, exercise, and preventive care. Medical information is not provided. Rather, participants are encouraged to foster a close relationship with their health care providers, and they are given instruction on how to manage this relationship efficiently.

Healthy Living: Managing Ongoing Health Conditions
Under the leadership of the Community Diabetes Project, these classes are made possible by the combined efforts of the following agencies: Saint James Clinic, Indian Health Center , San Jose Municipal Office on Aging, Kaiser Permanente-Santa Clara Health Education Department and Kaiser Permanente Community Benefit Program. These six-session series of classes are offered to English-speaking adults with health conditions such as heart disease, arthritis, diabetes, chronic obstructive pulmonary disease and multiple sclerosis. These classes are taught by trained volunteers facilitators once a week for 2.5 hours each time. Classes are group-oriented and highly interactive. Participants learn to fight fatigue and frustration, to communicate better with their health team, to manage stress, to deal with depression, develop an exercise program, set goals and problem solve to make changes in their lives. The classes are conducted at clinical or community settings usually in the morning from 9:30 to 12:00 Noon.
