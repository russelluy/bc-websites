Register Now! : 6th Annual Legislative Breakfast

In November 2004, voters passed Proposition 63. On January 1, 2005 it became state law and was renamed the Mental Health Services Act (MHSA). The MHSA vision is to use the funds to “transform the current mental health system in California...”

The MHSA imposes an additional 1% tax on that portion of a taxpayer's taxable income in excess of $1 million dollars. It is estimated that the new tax will raise in excess of $600 million dollars annually. Funding will be made annually from the Mental Health Services Fund to counties. Santa Clara County's share is between $17 - $30 million annually, depending on the state Department of Mental Health's allocation method.

Come join us on Friday, April 28 th to hear expert presentations on innovative ways to improve mental health services in Santa Clara County. The focus will be on integrated behavioral and primary health care. Register Now!
