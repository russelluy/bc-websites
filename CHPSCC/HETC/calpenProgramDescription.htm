California Preparedness Education Network(cal•PEN) Program Overview

cal•PEN is a collaboration between California 's Statewide Area Health Education Center Program (AHEC), and the California Poison Control System (CPCS), and is funded by a grant from the Federal Department of Health and Human Services.

This program will provide medical professionals across the state with emergency preparedness information for the following potential health threats:

  • Biological
  • Chemical
  • Nuclear
  • Incendiary or Explosive
  • Emerging Infectious Disease

Ultimately, the program will teach health professionals to recognize the indications of a terrorist event or other public health emergencies and will respond appropriately. The professionals will be trained to:

  • Alert the public health system at a local, state and national level for a coordinated universal response.
  • Participate in coordinated, multidisciplinary responses to events.

This program is specifically designed to build a network of skilled educators to conduct continuing education for those health professionals caring for residents of medically underserved communities.
