HETC Team Biographies

John Beleutz, MPH, Director: Health Education & Training Center
John Beleutz has directed the Health Education& Training Center since 2004. Mr. Beleutz oversees all HETC programs, and is director of the South Bay Area Health Education Center and San Jose AIDS Education and Training Center. His diverse experience includes: serving as a community health worker with the Peace Corps in Sierra Leone, directing an AIDS service organization in North Carolina, directing reproductive health programs in California and Oregon, and directing social marketing programs in Romania. Mr. Beleutz earned his Master of Public Health in Health Education and Health Behavior from the University of North Carolina, Chapel Hill in 1993.

Jennifer Shockey, MPH, Program Manager, Minority AIDS Initiative (MAI) and Trans-Powerment Program
Jennifer Shockey has worked with HETC since February 2002, where she currently manages the Minority AIDS Initiative and the Trans-Powerment Program. Ms. Shockey has worked in the field of HIV education and prevention for the past ten years and has worked with injection drug users, youth, and LGBT and sex workers. She has been a frontline outreach worker, an HIV test counselor, and a director of a prevention and education program. Ms. Shockey also acts as an expert faculty member for the San Jose AIDS Education & Training Center to train health care providers interested in improving their ability to serve transgender people. She earned her Master of Public Health from San Jose State University in 2004.

Ph. 408.289.9260 x214
Email. jen@chpscc.org

Lydia Guel, Program Manager, San Jose AIDS Education & Training Center and Area Health Education Center
Lydia Guel returned to her hometown of San Jose to join the HETC team in September 2005. Her extensive experience with program planning and evaluation, community-based event production and promotion, and social marketing is reflective of her diverse work with the County of Sonoma Department of Health Services since 2001 and previous 6 years in the field of prevention and planning related to youth advocacy, HIV/AIDS prevention, social marketing and alcohol, tobacco, and other drug-related programs. She completed her Bachelors of Arts in Community Health Education at the University of California at Berkeley in 2001.

Kirstin Hofmann Ruf, MNPL, Program Manager, California Preparedness Education Network
Kirstin Hofmann Ruf manages the South Bay cal-PEN (California Preparedness Education Network) program and supports clinic emergency preparedness activities in Santa Clara County. She was previously an Initiatives Manager with the United Way of King County in Seattle, Washington, supporting community engagement and volunteerism throughout King County. Prior to that, Ms. Ruf was the Community Outreach Coordinator with the American Red Cross of Seattle-King County, where she coordinated the HIV/AIDS program and health and safety education outreach education to underserved communities. Ms. Ruf is a disaster responder for the American Red Cross and led response efforts following the Nisqually earthquake in Washington State in 2001. Ms. Ruf earned her Masters of Not-for-Profit Leadership from Seattle University in 2005.

Ellie Anderson, Program Coordinator, California Preparedness Education Network
Ellie Anderson has been with Community Health Partnership since 2002 and has been working on the Partnership’s emergency preparedness programs since 2004. In her role as Program Coordinator, Ms. Anderson is responsible for marketing, program development, faculty coordination and implementation of the calPEN program in the San Francisco Bay Area region. Prior to joining the Partnership, Ms. Anderson completed an internship on disaster response at the American Red Cross, Santa Cara Valley Chapter, where she became a member of the Disaster Action Team. Ms. Anderson graduated Cum Laude from Santa Clara University in 2002, earning her Bachelor of Science degree with a major in Anthropology, and a specialization in Applied Anthropology.

Danielle R Castro, Health Educator, Case Manager, Trans-Powerment Program
Danielle Castro has volunteered in the field of HIV/AIDS prevention and education for over ten years. Her advocacy and outreach efforts have been predominantly related to transgender issues as she is trans-identified. Ms. Castro is a member of both the Santa Clara County HIV Prevention Community Planning Group and Santa Clara County HIV Health Services Planning Council; co-founder of both the Transgender Youth Group at the Billy De Frank Center and the San Jose Steering Committee for the Transgender Day of Remembrance; and recipient of the 2005 Human Relations Award for District 2 of the County of Santa Clara. She has worked with the San Jose AIDS Education and Training Center as an independent consultant on transgender health care issues since 2003, joined the HETC staff under the Trans-Powerment program in October 2005, and became the newest employee of the calPEN team in March 2005.
