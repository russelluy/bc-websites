California Preparedness Education Network
            

 

The South Bay calPEN program offers disaster preparedness education for healthcare professionals through FREE courses on the following topics:

Managing the Unknown: an Introduction to Disaster Preparedness
Module I

Bioterrorism Preparedness
Module II

Chemical Agents of Consequence
Module III

Emerging Infectious Diseases
Module IV

Infectious Diseases after Natural Disasters
Module V

Pandemic Influenza
Module VI

These programs are specifically intended for healthcare professionals, particularly those working with medically underserved communities. Click here to see a list of calPEN audiences. calPEN is the ideal choice for the busy healthcare professional looking to enhance awareness for themselves, their patients, and their communities:

- The entire program comes FREE of any charge to you through a grant from HRSA

- CMEs / CEUs / CNEs / CPEs are provided FREE, and units are received directly after the presentation*

- Local expert faculty visit your site at a time that is convenient for your staff

- All equipment and training materials are supplied and set up by calPEN staff

- Quality educational materials are regularly updated and are county-specific

- Each presentation lasts for one hour (an additional 15 minutes is recommended to provide ample time for Q & A)

- Any combination of topics can be arranged and they can be taken in any order

* Dental CEUs will be mailed out after the presentation

PARTNERS

calPEN is a program of the California Area Health Education Centers (AHECs) under HRSA grant TO1HP01405. It began in 2003 in collaboration with the California Poison Control System, (www.calpoison.org) and has evolved since that time to include partnerships with the California Academy of Family Practitioners (CAFP) and University of California Television (UCTV). calPEN is also recognized by the CDC (Centers for Disease Control and Prevention) (www.cdc.gov) and the California Department of Health Services (www.dhs.ca.gov)
