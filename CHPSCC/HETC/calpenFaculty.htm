calPEN Faculty Bios

Christian Sandrock, MD, MPH
calPEN Medical Director
Assistant Professor of Clinical Medicine
Division of Pulmonary/Critical Care Medicine
Division of Infectious Diseases
Department of Internal Medicine
University of California, Davis Medical Center

My expertise is in infectious diseases, pulmonary, and critical care medicine and I specialize in disaster preparedness, emerging infectious diseases, terrorism, and other threats to public health. As Medical Director of the California Preparedness Education Network (calPEN), I develop educational materials, focusing primarily on providers in rural, boarder, inner city and underserved areas of the state. I was prior Medical Director of the Hospital Bioterrorism Preparedness Program for the State of California and currently, as medical advisor to the Emergency Medical Services Authority (EMSA), I contribute my experience to the Center for Disease Control and Prevention, Hospital Bioterrorism Preparedness Program, and many other Homeland Security projects. In particular, I am currently working with the Department of Health Services and EMSA in pandemic influenza and other infectious disease outbreak planning, disease surveillance, and hospital infection control preparedness.

Daljeet Rai, MD
Associate Clinical Professor, Stanford University School of Medicine
O’Connor Hospital Family Practice Residency Program

I am an MD from University of Southern California, currently teaching in the San Jose-O'Connor Hospital Family Practice Residency Program. I have a faculty appointment at Stanford University School of Medicine as Associate Clinical Professor. I have worked internationally in India and Ecuador. I spent one month in Indonesia after the Tsunami and two weeks in Louisiana after hurricane Katrina.

David McKay, MD, MPH (epidemiology)
Diplomate, American boards of family medicine and internal medicine
Clinical Professor of Medicine, Stanford University
Faculty and attending physician, O'Connor Hospital Family Medicine Residency Program

I graduated Harvard Medical School and received my MPH in epidemiology from the University of North Carolina. I was an International Research Career Development Fellow of the National Institutes of Health and worked three years in Malaysia on epidemiologic research.

George Kent, MD
Associate Director, San Jose-O’Connor Hospital Family Medicine Residency Program
Physician, Ira Greene Positive PACE Clinic, Santa Clara Valley Medical Center
Clinical Professor, Family and Community Medicine, Stanford University
Diplomate, American Board of Family Practice

I graduated from the Case Western Reserve University School of Medicine and the University of California at San Francisco affiliated Family Practice Residency Program at Community Hospital of Sonoma County in Santa Rosa, CA. I also completed a Preventive Medicine Residency and served as a Medical Epidemiologist at the U.S. Centers for Disease Control in Atlanta, GA. Beyond my activities at the San Jose-O'Connor Hospital, I staff a primary care clinic for patients with HIV disease at the Santa Clara Valley Medical Center. I have international health experience in Latin America and Africa.

Kathryn Meier, PharmD DABAT
Toxicology Management Specialist, California Poison Control System
Assistant Clinical Professor of Pharmacy, UCSF School of Pharmacy

I currently work as a Toxicology Management Specialist, with the California Poison Control System, San Francisco Division and am an Assistant Clinical Professor of Pharmacy at the UCSF School of Pharmacy. I earned my doctor of pharmacy degree from the University of California at San Francisco, School of Pharmacy and completed my clinical pharmacy residency at the Veterans Administration Medical Center in Palo Alto, California. I am a California Board of Pharmacy licensed pharmacist; American Association of Poison Control Centers certified Specialist in Poison Information; and a Diplomat of the American Board of Applied Toxicology. I have experience in delivering continuing education courses and lectures, and my interest and expertise is in clinical toxicology and poison information.

Lisa S. Angell, RN, BSN, MICN
Paramedic/Pediatric Liaison Coordinator; SN IV. Emergency Department; Watsonville Community Hospital
Adjunct Faculty; Cabrillo College ADN Nursing Program

I have worked as a nurse for 28 years. My current job is in the Emergency Department where I split time as the Paramedic Base Station Coordinator and a staff nurse. I am also a member of the EMSA Disaster Interest Group, and have served on CDHS committees determining how HRSA bioterrorism funds will be distributed throughout the State. I have been in the USAF reserves as a flight nurse and was deployed in Desert Storm. I've served on the HEICS III committee and stay active and interested in emergency preparedness for the clinical provider. My specific expertise in disaster preparedness began in 1989 when the Loma Prieta earthquake rocked Santa Cruz County and rendered our community hospital unserviceable. Since that night, bringing the message about the issues of disaster readiness have been my personal and professional goals.

Marianne Garvey, RN, BSN
Clinical Nurse, Physiological Nursing, UCSF School of Nursing

I have twenty-seven years of nursing and teaching experience internationally and domestically. I have worked with calPEN and emergency preparedness for the last 3 years, first as program coordinator for the South Bay AHEC, then as Curriculum Coordinator working to standardize the calPEN Modules and coordinate the first Faculty Development Conference in January 2006, and now as calPEN Faculty. My experience in emergency preparedness includes being a Red Cross Nurse, a CERT member in Newark, a past member of Santa Clara County DPH Mass Prophylaxis Workgroup, as well as participation in several full scale, tabletop and functional exercises. My recent experience has included surveying 45 clinics in Santa Clara County on their emergency preparedness plans and readiness.

Shelley Salpeter, MD, FACP
Clinical Professor of Medicine, Stanford University School of Medicine
Director of Medical Consultation Services, Santa Clara Valley Medical Center

I am Clinical Professor of Medicine at Stanford University School of Medicine and Director of the Medicine Consultation Services and Santa Clara Valley Medical Center. I also work closely with the Santa Clara Department of Public Health in order to educate physicians in matters of public health importance. My special interests are physician preparedness for bioterrorism and chemical terrorism and pandemic influenza.

Thuy Thu Hoang, M.D.
Board Certified Internal Medicine, focus in Preventive Medicine
Private Practice in San Jose

I received my BA in Biochemistry at UC Berkeley, grew up in San Francisco, Berkeley, and have been living in San Jose since 1992. I graduated from New York Medical College in 1992, and completed my residency training at the Santa Clara Valley Medical Center in San Jose. I have been in private practice for 8 years and work part-time occasionally for one of the county medical clinics (Chaboya Clinic of Santa Clara Valley Health & Hospital System since 1994). I have close ties/strong networking with the Vietnamese-American community in the Bay Area and the Vietnamese media. I have been a frequent guest speaker on Vietnamese radio and television programs since 2001.

William C. Fowkes, MD
Medical Director, Pathways Hospice and Hospice of the Valley
Chair, Medical Committee, South Skyline Emergency Preparedness Organization
Member, Board of Directors, South Skyline Emergency Preparedness Organization

I am an Emeritus Professor of Medicine, Stanford University School of Medicine. My specialties are Internal Medicine, Family practice, with added Qualifications in Geriatrics. I serve on the Board of Directors South Skyline Emergency Preparedness Organization and Chair of the Medical Committee. I am the Medical Director, Pathways Hospice and Hospice of the Valley and the former Chair of the Division of Family and Community Medicine, Stanford. I am also the founder and former director of the San Jose Hospital (now San Jose O'Connor) Family Practice Residency.
