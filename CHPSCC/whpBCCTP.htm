Women's Health Partnership: Breast and Cervical Cancer Treatment Program (BCCTP)

The treatment program began on January 1, 2002 to provide services for women and men with breast cancer and women with cervical cancer. To qualify for BCCTP the patients must meet the following criteria

•  Low-income
•  California residents who have been served by California 's screening programs and have been determined to need treatment, and
•  Are uninsured or underinsured for that treatment

Patients who do not meet the federal requirements for full scope Medi-Cal benefits may still be eligible for the State-only funded component, which will provide Medi-Cal breast cancer treatment benefits for 18 months or cervical cancer treatment benefits for 24 months. The State-only program covers individuals 65 and over, individuals with “unsatisfactory” immigration status, and individuals with other credible health insurance with patient co-insurance cost of $750 or more.

The Web based BCCTP application can provide “real-time” Medi-Cal eligibility determination when completed by a Family PACT, or CDP provider at http://www.medi-cal.ca.gov. Eligibility specialists for this program are available in English and Spanish. Call Monday – Friday from 8am to 5pm at 1-800-824-0088.

The California Health Collaborative is offering clinical care coordination and patient navigation services for breast and cervical cancer patients eligible or enrolled with Medi-Cal and BCCTP.  Call toll-free 1-888-921-7465 or view their website at www.healthcollaborative.org
