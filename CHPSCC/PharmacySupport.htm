Pharmacy Support

The Pharmaceutical Access Program was implemented with the goal of assisting community health centers in adopting programs to provide low-income patients with options for obtaining low-cost and no-cost pharmaceuticals.

Pharmaceutical Access Programs include:

  • 340B – The result of federal legislation passed in 1992 that requires drug manufacturers to provide outpatient pharmaceuticals to qualifying health care entities at a significant discount.
  • Patient Assistance Programs – Programs provided by pharmacy manufacturers that provides free drugs to patients who meet specific eligibility criteria.
  • Dispensaries – Requires a license for a M.D. to stock medication and to provide medications or samples to eligible patients.
