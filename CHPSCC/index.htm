___The second largest HIV/AIDS fundraiser in the south bay (after the Walk for AIDS) is coming up.

Unmask the Mysteries Mardi Gras Gala is scheduled for 7:00 pm Saturday, May 20 th,  2006 at the  Triton Museum of Art in Santa Clara. Building on last year's very successful event the AIDS Coalition is turning the masked ball into an annual fundraiser for local HIV/AIDS groups. The 2005 inaugural event was attended by over 300 people and raised more than $80,000 for local AIDS agencies.

Unmask the Mysteries is organized around a New Orleans Mardi Gras theme and guests are urged to come in masks or costumes . In addition, one of a kind masks are created and contributed for the event, some by celebrities such as Patty LuPone and the cast of "Queer Eye for the Straight Guy”, who donated last year. This year's event will also include a silent auction where some of the masks created by celebrities will be sold.

ACSV expects that the cost of organizing the event will be covered entirely by corporate sponsors so all ticket revenue goes entirely to the benefiting groups. There are nine benefiting organizations: AIDS Emergency Fund, AIDS Legal Services, ACSV Education and Advocacy Project. Asian Americans for Community Involvement, Bill Wilson Center, Billy DeFrank LGBT Community Center, Health Trust AIDS Services, Health Education & Training Center and ProLatino of San Jose.

For more information about Unmask the Mysteries or to purchase tickets please go to www.unmaskthemysteries.com or call 408-451-9255. Only 400 tickets are available for this year's gala and it is expected to sell out early.

For more information on AIDS Coaltion Silicon Valley goto www.AIDSCoalition.org.
