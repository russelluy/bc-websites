Community Health Partnership Programs

Manage a variety of programs for community health centers:

  • Women's Health Partnership
    • Neighborhood Health Days - Coordinate a series of contywide health fairs geared towards medically underserved women in their middle years. Each Health Day is tailored to meet the specific health, language, and cultural needs of each community, reaching women where they live.
    • Women's Health Resouce Directory - Develop and distribute a health guide to low- and no-cost services in Santa Clara County. The Women's Health Resource Directory is printed in English, Chinese, Spanish, and Vietnamese.
    • Healthy Lifestyles Curriculum - Provide high quality health education on breast health, menopause/osteoporosis, nutrition, physical activity, and stress management to medically underserved, uninsured, low-income women age 40 and above.
  • Health Education and Training Center
    • HIV/AIDS Education and Prevention - The San Jose AIDS Education and Training Center (SJAETC) provides health care workers in the Santa Clara, Salinas, and Monterey Bay areas with AIDS-related training, education and information services while El Pueblo Against AIDS focuses on HIV prevention and education
    • California Preparedness Education Network(cal•PEN) - A collaboration between California 's Statewide Area Health Education Center Program (AHEC), and the California Poison Control System (CPCS), and is funded by a grant from the Federal Department of Health and Human Services.
    • Health Professions Development - Health Education and Training Center is one of 175 centers affiliated with the national and California Area Health Education Center (AHEC) systems. The Center was founded in 1981 as an AHEC with support from the Stanford University School of Medicine and San José Medical Center and funding from the California AHEC.
  • Community Diabetes Project
    • Self Management Classes - Increase individuals' ability to manage their diabetes and minimize the long term complications of their condition through a free diabetes education and self-management program.
    • Diabetes Supply Bank - Provide free blood glucose testing supplies to medically underserved diabetics.
    • Fiestabetes - Provide ongoing Spanish language support and education for self-management program graduates.
  • Pediatric Weight Management Program
    • In collaboration with the Lucile Packard Children's Hospital, the Community Diabetes Project of the Community Health Partnership is currently offering the Packard Weight Control Program, a family-based, behavioral and educational program designed to promote healthy eating and exercise habits for overweight children between the ages of 8-12 and their families.
  • Health Systems Network
    • Provide services to community health centers to increase their business practices and quality of service delivery. This is accomplished through various technical and collaborative activities with the County Health System.
