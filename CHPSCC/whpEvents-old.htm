Women's Health Partnership: Upcoming Events

7th Annual Cancer Lunch Symposium

 Cancer Through A Different Lens: Innovative Approaches to Cancer Prevention and Treatment in Underserved Populations

Featuring:

Evaon Wong-Kim, PhD, MPH, LCSW, Associate Professor at the Department of Social Work, California State University East Bay. Dr. Wong-Kim is a founding member of the Intercultural Cancer Council, a national advocacy organization for improving the cancer mortality rate of the underserved populations.

Beverly Burns, MS, LA , Traditional Chinese Medicine/Integrative Medicine Specialist, Osher Center for Integrative Medicine, U.C. San Francisco. Ms. Burns is a co-founder and Clinical Director for the Charlotte Maxwell Complementary Clinic in Oakland , a clinic for low-income women with cancer.

Moderated By:

Ysabel Duron, Founder and Executive Director of Latinas Contra Cancer and Co-Anchor of KRON 4 Weekend Morning News.

Objectives:

By end of symposium, participants will be able to:

  • Discuss the implications of multicultural cancer research in advocating for underserved populations.
  • Describe the role of complementary and alternative medicine in treating underserved women with cancer.
  • Identify at least 3 cancer prevention or treatment approaches in serving underserved populations.

This Symposium is dedicated to the memory of Jane Gremmel, who was a public health nurse and pioneer in advocating for women's health issues in Santa Clara County as well as a staunch supporter of the Women's Health Partnership program.

The Women's Health Partnership promotes health and wellness, and improves awareness of and access to healthcare for medically underserved women in their middle years.
