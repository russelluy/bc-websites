Interviewing Guidelines

Hiring the right individual for the position is one of the most crucial decisions you can make in helping you company or business grow properly. That being said, it is a challenging task to match a prospective candidate with the job but it is a worthwhile investment of time and effort. To ensure that you hire the most qualified individuals consider the following tips:

   Make a list of questions ahead of time that you wish to ask about the candidate’s resume. Use open-ended questions (those that prevent YES or NO answer but require an explanation).
   Always do your best o make the candidates feel comfortable; after all, they are still your guests.
   Be sure you are clear on the requirements, especially the most crucial ones and that you can convey them clearly.
   Make sure you are and remain in control of the interview, but allow the candidate to ask questions, especially after the interview.
   Develop and ask interview questions that effectively determine the presence or absence of a skill. This task can be left up a second interviewer who may be more familiar with the specific skills required for a given position.
   The purpose of the interview is to screen job candidates and predict the candidate’s success if employed. Keep in mind an old rule of thumb: “the best predictor of the future is the past”. Specific questions that probe deeper their past experience can reveal potential areas of concern; however, they often reveal positive information that the candidate may have inadvertently left out or felt was not pertinent to the position.
   Don’t use your interview as the sole criterion for selection or rejection. A second interviewer can be of great help, especially if they are closer to the position requirements. Also, the appropriate test can divulge information about an individual’s work ethics, sales skills, etc… There are many I the market with several of them providing for a strong “second opinion”.
   Never discard the importance of understanding the candidate as a person. There are many personal qualities that may transfer directly and positively into job skills and the right as well as appropriate questions can help you determine if there is a fit. *
   Be fair in your interviewing practices.
   Without being committal, close the interview and let the candidate know when you will be in touch with a response.
   Following the interview and while you memory is fresh, make a few notes or edit the ones you took.

Be sure to stay within Federal guidelines with regard to non-permissible interview questions.
