INTERVIEWING TIPS

   Remember the 5 P’s: “Proper Preparation Prevents Poor Performance”. It isn’t difficult to ask or find a web site address prior to our interview to learn about the business and industry you are considering for employment. If time allows, call and ask for product brochures or company literature or even Annual Reports if available. Besides, if you are applying for a sales position, the interviewer (likely to be your next sales manager or VP of Sales) will be impressed and reassured that he/she is considering somone who prepares for presentations.

Keep in mind that YOU are also interviewing the company you are applying to. It has to be a fit for you as well, not just the other way around. So, the more you know about them, the better you will interview and the more confidence you will have when you say YES, I accept the challenge.

If you compiled your resume properly, it should have highlights from your previous positions as they apply to the position you are seeking. Be prepared to discuss those accomplishments quantitatively and qualitatively. This is a must if you want to stand out from the rest. DO NOT assume that the interviewer will ask; all interviewers have their own personal style of conducting an interview and many are not as savvy at the task as you might think.

Good personal skills are always appreciated and in demand in today's organizations; they are expected in sales and marketing related positions. Remind yourself before every interview to present yourself through your posture, gestures, facial expressions, eye contact, choice of words, speed of speech, etc. exactly as you want to be seen.

What about attire? Though we have seen a change in it in recent years, an interview is still an interview and you are better off making an error on the conservative side. A professional suit for men and women is still the best choice for positions in sales and marketing. If you are unsure as to how formal or contemporary, you can always call anonymously into the company and ask someone to guide you as to the corporate environment. Keep in mind, however, that you are asking for an opinion.

Additional Tips

• If you have not interviewed in a while, be aware that you will get better with practice. But, you may want to rehearse with a friend since the position of choice may very well be the first or second company you apply to.
• Never interrupt the interviewer and always wait for him/her to finish the question. It isn’t just a matter of courtesy, it will prevent you answering the wrong question
• Don’t ask just good questions (your competition will do that too) ask great questions. And, since you are in a sales related position, ask questions as if you were speaking to a client, each one bringing you nearer to the close. Use questions such as: When can we meet again or who will I meet with next and when would be a good time? How do my qualifications compare with other candidates’?
• When the time is right and if the interview went well, don’t forget to Ask for the Job just as you would ask for the order after a sales presentation!
• Don’t forget to thank the interviewer for his/her time and consideration and then do something most people forget to do (even in sales), send a Thank You note even if you are not too sure you did very well.
