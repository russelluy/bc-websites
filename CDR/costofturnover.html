Cost of Turnover

Hiring Managers often fail to realize that turnover carries a huge price followed by enormous costs - some obvious, others not so obvious.

OBVIOUS
The actual price of hiring a replacement: advertising (newspaper ads, internet ads, industry publications ads, etc…), recruiting fees (these can be high depending on the position being filled and if you should fall in the hands of the wrong recruiting firm).

NOT SO OBVIOUS
Time away from more revenue generating tasks: screening time, interviewing time, reference and background checks, aptitude testing, etc… What about the lost revenue from a territory that is not being worked?

OFTEN NOT OBVIOUS AT ALL
Low employee and department morale, possible litigation, unemployment compensation, new candidates ask about turnover and develop the wrong opinion of your business). Low productivity and poor customer service prior to leaving can often occur months before it is detected. This is especially costly when the “unhappy” individual is in sales and should be generating revenues.
